## The Quick Answer: How Old Is Your Roof?

Here's a general rule of thumb based on roofing material:

| Roof Type | Expected Lifespan | When to Start Watching |
|-----------|------------------|----------------------|
| 3-Tab Asphalt Shingles | 15-20 years | After 12 years |
| Architectural Shingles | 25-30 years | After 20 years |
| Premium/Designer Shingles | 30-50 years | After 25 years |
| Metal Roofing | 40-70 years | After 35 years |
| Tile/Slate | 50-100 years | After 40 years |
| Wood Shakes | 20-25 years | After 15 years |

**But here's the thing:** Charlotte's weather can shorten these lifespans. Our humidity promotes algae growth. Our hailstorms cause impact damage. Our temperature swings stress materials. A roof that might last 30 years in a milder climate might only make it 22-25 years here.

## 9 Warning Signs Your Roof Needs Attention

Before I tell you when to replace, let me tell you what to look for. You can spot most of these from the ground or your attic:

### 1. Shingles Are Curling or Cupping
When shingles curl up at the edges or cup in the middle, they've lost their flexibility. Water gets underneath, and it's only a matter of time before you have leaks.

**What it means:** If curling is widespread, you're looking at replacement within 1-3 years. If it's just a few shingles, repair might work.

### 2. Missing Shingles
Wind, age, or poor installation can cause shingles to blow off. One or two missing shingles? Easy repair. Missing shingles in multiple areas? That's a sign of systemic failure.

**What it means:** Isolated missing shingles = repair. Pattern of missing shingles = start planning for replacement.

### 3. Granules in Your Gutters
Those little sand-like granules protect your shingles from UV damage. When they start coming off in large quantities, your shingles are deteriorating.

**What it means:** Some granule loss is normal, especially on new roofs. Heavy accumulation in gutters (especially after 15+ years) means your roof is aging out.

### 4. Cracked or Broken Shingles
Thermal cycling - hot days followed by cool nights - causes shingles to expand and contract. Over time, this leads to cracking.

**What it means:** A few cracked shingles = repair. Widespread cracking = replacement needed soon.

### 5. Dark Streaks or Stains
Those black streaks aren't just ugly - they're algae that's actually eating your shingles. Welcome to Charlotte's humidity.

**What it means:** Algae alone doesn't require replacement. But it accelerates wear. Consider cleaning, and next time, choose algae-resistant shingles.

### 6. Daylight Through the Roof Boards
Go into your attic on a sunny day. Turn off the lights. If you see pinpoints of light coming through, you have holes.

**What it means:** This is serious. If you see daylight, you likely have water coming in too. Call someone today.

### 7. Sagging Areas
A roof should have straight, even lines. If you see dips, waves, or sagging sections, the structure underneath is compromised.

**What it means:** This requires immediate attention. Sagging can indicate rotting decking, structural issues, or excessive weight. Don't wait.

### 8. Higher Energy Bills
If your heating and cooling costs keep climbing, poor roof ventilation or insulation could be the culprit.

**What it means:** Might not require full replacement, but definitely needs inspection. Sometimes adding ventilation or improving attic insulation solves it.

### 9. Moss or Plant Growth
Unlike algae, moss actually holds moisture against your roof. In Charlotte's climate, it can cause real damage.

**What it means:** Moss needs to be removed carefully (pressure washing damages shingles). If the growth is extensive and has been there for years, check for underlying damage.

## The Inspection Checklist: What I Look For

When I inspect a roof, here's my process. You can do most of this yourself:

### From the Ground:
- [ ] Overall shingle condition (curling, missing, cracking)
- [ ] Roof lines (any sagging or waves?)
- [ ] Flashing condition around chimney and vents
- [ ] Gutter condition (sagging, pulling away?)
- [ ] Debris accumulation in valleys

### From the Attic:
- [ ] Daylight visible through roof boards
- [ ] Water stains on rafters or decking
- [ ] Mold or mildew smell
- [ ] Proper ventilation (can you feel air movement?)
- [ ] Insulation condition

### After a Storm:
- [ ] Dented gutters or downspouts (indicates hail)
- [ ] Granules washed into landscaping
- [ ] Dented metal flashing or vents
- [ ] Any new leaks, even small ones

## When Repair Makes More Sense Than Replacement

Here's something most roofers won't tell you: **not every problem requires a new roof.**

I recommend repair when:

1. **Your roof is under 15 years old** and the damage is localized
2. **The problem is limited to one area** (one slope, around one penetration)
3. **Repair costs are less than 30%** of replacement cost
4. **There's no structural damage** to the decking beneath
5. **You're planning to sell soon** and just need to get a few more years

I recommend replacement when:

1. **Your roof is 20+ years old** and showing wear
2. **You've had multiple repairs** in the past few years
3. **Damage is widespread** across multiple areas
4. **Decking is damaged** and needs replacement
5. **You're seeing interior water damage** in multiple locations
