### 5. How to Choose the Right Shingle Color

"Here's my process when helping Charlotte homeowners choose:

**Step 1: Look at Your Fixed Elements**
- Brick color (can't change it)
- Stone accents
- Front door color
- Driveway/walkway

**Step 2: Consider Your Siding**
- White/cream: Almost any shingle works
- Gray: Stick to gray/black shingles
- Warm tones: Avoid cool grays

**Step 3: Check Your Neighbors**
- Not to copy them
- To ensure you don't clash
- HOA may have guidelines

**Step 4: Get Actual Samples**
- Online colors are misleading
- View samples in sunlight
- Place against your home's exterior

**Step 5: Think About Resale**
- Bold colors limit buyer appeal
- Neutrals maximize resale value
- #1 regret I hear: 'I wish I'd gone more neutral'"

### 8. FAQ Section

**Q: What is the most popular CertainTeed shingle color?**
A: Weathered Wood is the #1 CertainTeed Landmark color nationally and in Charlotte. Its versatile brown-gray blend works with most home exteriors and remains timeless.

**Q: What shingle color is best for resale value?**
A: Neutral colors like Weathered Wood, Driftwood, Pewter, and Moire Black maximize resale appeal. Avoid bold or unusual colors if you plan to sell within 5-7 years.

**Q: How do I match shingle color to my brick?**
A: Look for shingle colors that pick up a secondary tone in your brick. For red brick, try Weathered Wood or Burnt Sienna. For tan brick, Driftwood works well. Avoid exact matches - slight contrast is more appealing.

**Q: Do darker shingles make my house hotter?**
A: Slightly, but modern shingles with reflective granules minimize heat absorption. The difference between Driftwood and Moire Black is typically 2-5°F in attic temperature with proper ventilation.

**Q: Can I see shingle colors in person before deciding?**
A: Yes! We bring full-size shingle samples to your home. Viewing samples against your actual siding and in your home's lighting is the only way to be confident in your choice.

**Q: What CertainTeed color is trending in 2026?**
A: Georgetown Gray and Moire Black are gaining popularity in Charlotte. The trend toward cooler tones and higher contrast is driving these choices, especially in newer neighborhoods.
