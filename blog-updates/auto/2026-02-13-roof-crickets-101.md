**What is a Roof Cricket?**
A roof cricket (also called a saddle or diverter) is a peaked structure built behind chimneys or other roof penetrations to divert water and debris away from the vulnerable joint. Required by code in Charlotte for chimneys wider than 30 inches on the uphill side of the roof slope.

### 5. Expanded FAQ Section

**Q: What is a roof cricket and why do I need one?**
A: A roof cricket is a small peaked structure that diverts water and debris away from chimneys and other roof penetrations. Without one, water pools against the chimney, eventually working under the flashing and causing leaks and rot.

**Q: How much does roof cricket installation cost in Charlotte?**
A: In 2026, expect $450-$2,000 depending on size and complexity. During a full roof replacement, add-on pricing is typically 30-40% lower. Most Charlotte chimney crickets run $750-$1,200 installed.

**Q: Can I add a roof cricket to my existing roof?**
A: Yes, crickets can be retrofitted. We'll need to remove shingles around the chimney, build the cricket structure, install new underlayment and shingles, and properly integrate the flashing. Budget $1,000-$2,500 for a retrofit.

**Q: What size cricket do I need for my chimney?**
A: Cricket width should match your chimney width. The cricket should extend at least 12" up the roof from the chimney. Pitch should be at least 3:12 to shed water effectively.

**Q: Do all chimneys need roof crickets?**
A: Not all - but most larger chimneys do. NC code requires crickets for chimneys over 30" wide on the uphill side. Even smaller chimneys benefit from crickets if you're seeing debris buildup or water staining.

**Q: What's the difference between a roof cricket and a saddle?**
A: They're the same thing. "Cricket" is more common in the South, while "saddle" is used more in the Northeast. Some roofers also call them "diverters."
