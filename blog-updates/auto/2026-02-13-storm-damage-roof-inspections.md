### 4. What Professional Inspectors Look For

"When I do a storm damage inspection, I'm checking things you can't see from the ground:

**Shingle Damage Indicators:**
- Hail hits that expose black mat (the fiberglass under granules)
- Soft spots in shingle that bruise when pressed
- Random hit pattern (versus aligned cracking from age)
- Granule displacement creating 'splatter' patterns

**Not All Damage is Obvious:**
Hail can damage shingles without leaving visible marks. I use a specific technique - pressing firmly on the shingle surface. Storm-damaged shingles will feel soft or spongy where the mat beneath was fractured. These will fail within 1-3 years even though they look fine today.

**What I Document:**
- Chalk-marked damage locations for photos
- Number of hits per 10x10 test square
- Soft metal damage as 'hail markers'
- Gutter splatter patterns
- Timestamp and weather data from storm"

### 7. FAQ Section

**Q: How do I inspect my roof for hail damage?**
A: From the ground, look for dented gutters, damaged vents, and missing shingles. Check soft metals like AC units. Do NOT get on your roof - call a professional for a free inspection after any hail storm.

**Q: How soon after a storm should I get a roof inspection?**
A: Within 30 days is ideal for documentation purposes. However, insurance claims are typically valid for up to 1 year. The sooner you document damage, the stronger your claim.

**Q: Will my insurance cover hail damage roof repair in Lake Norman?**
A: Most homeowner policies cover hail damage. You'll pay your deductible (typically $1,000-$2,500), and insurance covers the rest. We help Lake Norman homeowners navigate claims daily.

**Q: What size hail causes roof damage?**
A: Hail 1" (quarter-sized) and larger typically causes shingle damage. However, wind-driven smaller hail can also cause damage. We see claims approved for 3/4" hail regularly.

**Q: How can I tell the difference between hail damage and normal wear?**
A: Hail damage creates random circular dents across the entire roof and damages soft metals (vents, flashing). Normal wear shows gradual deterioration concentrated on sun-facing slopes. A professional can tell the difference.

**Q: Do I need to get my roof replaced after hail damage?**
A: Not always. Minor hail damage can sometimes be repaired. If 25%+ of your roof is damaged, or if the damage compromises the shingle's waterproofing, replacement is typically the better option.
