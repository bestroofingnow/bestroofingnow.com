### 4. How to Evaluate Roofing Company Reviews

"When you're comparing roofers, here's what I'd tell my own family to look for:

**Red Flags in Reviews:**
- All reviews are 5 stars from the same week (suspicious)
- No recent reviews (company may have quality issues or be inactive)
- Reviews don't mention specific crew members or work details (may be fake)
- Company responds defensively to negative reviews

**Green Flags in Reviews:**
- Mix of 4 and 5 star reviews (nobody's perfect)
- Detailed descriptions of work performed
- Mentions of specific people by name
- Company responds professionally to any concerns
- Reviews spread across months/years showing consistent quality

**Where to Look:**
- Google Business Profile (most verified)
- Facebook (easy to see reviewer profiles)
- Better Business Bureau (complaints, not just reviews)
- Angi / HomeAdvisor (if company is listed)

Don't just look at star ratings - read what people actually say."

### 8. FAQ Section

**Q: How do I find a top-rated roofing company in Charlotte?**
A: Start with Google reviews for Charlotte roofers, verify their NC contractor license, check BBB ratings, and request written estimates from at least 3 companies. Look for detailed reviews that mention specific work, not just star ratings.

**Q: What should I look for in roofing company reviews?**
A: Look for: recent reviews (within 6 months), specific details about work performed, mentions of communication quality, and how the company handled any problems. Be cautious of companies with only perfect 5-star reviews - genuine review profiles have some variation.

**Q: How do I verify a Charlotte roofing company is legitimate?**
A: Verify their NC General Contractor license at nclbgc.org, request certificates of insurance, check Google and BBB reviews, and ask for local references. Legitimate companies readily provide this information.

**Q: Why do some roofing companies have bad reviews?**
