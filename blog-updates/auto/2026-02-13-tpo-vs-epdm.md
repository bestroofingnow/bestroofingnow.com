### 4. Charlotte-Specific Considerations

"Choosing between TPO, EPDM, and modified bitumen for your Charlotte building? Here's what matters in our climate:

**Charlotte's Climate Factors:**

- **UV Exposure:** Our southern latitude means intense UV. This matters more than you'd think.
  - TPO: Excellent UV resistance, stays reflective
  - EPDM: Good UV resistance (black absorbs heat, but material handles it)
  - Mod Bit: Good UV resistance, may need recoating after 10-15 years

- **Summer Heat:** Average 90°F+ days: 80-90 per year
  - TPO's reflectivity saves real money on cooling
  - A 20,000 SF warehouse can save $3,000-5,000/year on cooling with white TPO vs dark EPDM

- **Storm Exposure:** Charlotte averages 8-12 significant storms per year
  - TPO seams are heat-welded - excellent wind resistance
  - EPDM seams use adhesive/tape - can fail in high winds if not properly installed
  - Mod Bit multi-layer = excellent overall storm resistance

- **Ponding Water:** Flat roofs pond water - it's unavoidable
  - EPDM handles ponding best (rubber doesn't degrade in standing water)
  - TPO handles ponding well with proper membrane
  - Mod Bit can have issues if ponding is severe (granule loss)"

### 7. Installation Method Differences

**TPO Installation:**
- Seams welded with hot air (minimum 800°F)
- Mechanically attached or fully adhered
- Can be installed in most weather (not rain)
- Faster installation for large areas

**EPDM Installation:**
- Seams bonded with adhesive or seam tape
- Fully adhered, mechanically attached, or ballasted
- More forgiving of temperature variations
- Requires proper surface prep for adhesives

**Modified Bitumen Installation:**
- Torch-applied (open flame)
- Cold-applied (adhesive)
- Self-adhering (peel and stick)
- More labor-intensive than single-ply

**Charlotte Installation Note:**
"Torch-applied mod bit requires fire permits and safety protocols. Some Charlotte buildings (especially close to other structures) may have restrictions. Self-adhered mod bit is a good alternative where torching isn't practical."

### 8. FAQ Section

**Q: What's the difference between TPO and modified bitumen roofing?**
A: TPO is a single-ply white membrane that's heat-welded at seams, known for energy efficiency. Modified bitumen is a multi-layer asphalt-based system with a granule surface, known for durability and foot traffic resistance.

**Q: Which is better for a Charlotte commercial building, TPO or EPDM?**
A: For most Charlotte buildings, TPO's energy savings (15-25% cooling reduction) make it the better long-term value. EPDM costs less upfront and is excellent for lower-profile buildings where aesthetics aren't a concern.

**Q: How long does a modified bitumen roof last in North Carolina?**
A: With proper installation and maintenance, modified bitumen lasts 15-20 years in Charlotte. Regular inspections and recoating every 10-12 years can extend life to 25+ years.

**Q: Is TPO or EPDM better for flat roofs with ponding water?**
A: EPDM handles ponding water better - rubber doesn't degrade in standing water. TPO also handles ponding well but requires proper membrane quality. Neither should have standing water for more than 48 hours after rain.

**Q: Which flat roof system has the best warranty?**
A: TPO typically offers the longest warranties (up to 25-30 years from major manufacturers). However, EPDM has been around longer with a proven 30+ year track record. Warranty terms matter more than length.

**Q: What's the most cost-effective commercial flat roof for a large warehouse?**
A: For warehouses over 20,000 SF in Charlotte, EPDM typically offers the best cost-per-square-foot value ($5-7/SF installed). For long-term energy savings, TPO pays back the higher cost in 5-7 years through cooling savings.
