### 1. Featured Snippet - Quick Answer Box

**When should you replace your roof?**
Replace your roof when you see: shingles that are 20+ years old, widespread granule loss, multiple leaks, sagging sections, or daylight through the roof deck. If repairs exceed 30% of replacement cost, replacement makes more financial sense.

### 2. The 9 Warning Signs Section (Expanded)

"As a Charlotte roofer, I get asked 'when should I replace my roof' at least once a day. Here's my honest breakdown of the signs I look for:

**1. Age of Your Roof**
- Architectural shingles: 25-30 years max
- 3-tab shingles: 15-20 years max
- If you bought your home without knowing the roof age, check permit records at Mecklenburg County

**2. Widespread Granule Loss**
- Check gutters for granule buildup (some is normal, excessive is not)
- Bald spots visible from ground = shingles are failing
- Charlotte's UV exposure accelerates granule loss

**3. Curling or Buckling Shingles**
- Curling edges = moisture damage or poor ventilation
- Buckling in middle = underlayment issues
- More than 10% affected = time to replace

**4. Multiple Active Leaks**
- One leak = repair
- Multiple leaks in different areas = underlying failure
- Water stains on multiple ceilings = system-wide problem

**5. Sagging Roof Deck**
- Visible dip or sag when viewing roofline
- Soft spots when walking on roof (don't do this yourself)
- This is structural - don't wait

**6. Daylight Through Roof Boards**
- Check attic on sunny day
- Any visible light = gaps in your protection
- Often accompanied by water stains

**7. Rising Energy Bills**
- Sudden increase with no explanation
- Poor roof ventilation or insulation failure
- Roof replacement = opportunity to fix both

**8. Storm Damage to 25%+ of Roof**
- Insurance typically covers this
- If damage is widespread, partial repair rarely makes sense
- Charlotte hail and wind storms often warrant full replacement

**9. You're Selling Your Home**
- Roof condition is top concern for buyers and inspectors
- 15+ year old roof will hurt your sale price
- Consider replacement for ROI (65-70% return)"

### 6. FAQ Section

**Q: When should I replace my roof vs repair it?**
A: Replace when: roof is 20+ years old, repairs would exceed 30% of replacement cost, you have multiple leak points, or there's visible sagging. Repair when: damage is localized, roof is under 15 years old, and the rest of the system is sound.

**Q: How do I know when to replace a roof if I don't know its age?**
A: Check closing documents from your home purchase, look up permit records at Mecklenburg County, or have a roofer inspect. We can often estimate age within 5 years based on shingle style, condition, and installation methods.

**Q: How often do roofs need to be replaced in Charlotte?**
A: Architectural shingles last 25-30 years, 3-tab shingles 15-20 years. Charlotte's weather (UV, humidity, storms) can shorten lifespan by 3-5 years compared to northern climates.

**Q: What time of year is best to replace a roof in Charlotte?**
A: Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are ideal - moderate temperatures for proper shingle sealing, less rain than summer, and crews are available. Avoid peak storm season (June-August) if possible.

**Q: Will my insurance cover roof replacement?**
A: Insurance covers storm damage, not wear and tear. If hail or wind damaged 25%+ of your roof, file a claim. If it's simply old, that's maintenance. I can help document storm damage for your claim.

**Q: How much does roof replacement cost in Charlotte in 2026?**
A: For a typical 2,000 SF Charlotte home: $14,000-$18,000 for quality architectural shingles, $18,000-$24,000 for premium options. Price depends on roof complexity, access, and material choice.
